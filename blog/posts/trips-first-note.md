Recently, I visited Yosemite as one of the last trips with friends in California before I move to NYC and it was an amazing experience. I personally received very weak reception so I couldn't use my phone at all. Since I was forced to live in the present, I was focused ont the natures beauty the entire time. 

![Yosemite valley view](assets/Yosemite1.jpeg)

Anywhere you go, the views were just spectacular. It's amazing how tall those granite cliffs were, that in the photo you can see a blue hue on them just because they were so far. I think its also strange how much of a difference elevation can make a place look. Its like entering a mystical place just driving into the valley.

Anyways, we camped here. It was great. Surprisingly not as cold as I thought it would be. I was a bit scared of making contact with bears, but I learned that they are actaully pretty shy and I never saw one which was awesome. I really enjoyed waking up and getting ready for the day. I wake up early so it was nice being alone in the bathroom washing my teeth. The whole process was very primitive feeling and therapeutic. 

![Yosemite campsite](assets/Yosemite2.jpeg)

The only thing that I disliked was cleaning and showering since it was cold when I went there and the water was either very cold, or very hot. We ate some pretty good food from home which included tacos and rice with some salsa and onions. Very delicious. But cleaning the pans and stove in freezing weather with cold water was hand numbing.

![Food review](assets/Yosemite3.jpeg)

We only went on one hike which was the Mist Trail and it was beutiful and kind of scary. The waterfall was enourmas and at the end was a rainbow. The steps were wet from the mist so it was sliperry and some places did not have any rails.

![Mist Trail waterfall](assets/Yosemite4.jpeg)

In the end, it was worth it. We got some pretty nice views at just how large this place is. That same rainbow can be seen here with tiny looking humans at bottom of the waterfall.

![Waterfall and rainbow at Yosemite](assets/Yosemite5.jpeg)

Overall, this has been the most beautiful places I have ever been to nature wise. I wish I took more photos but I was just too focused on the beatuy in person. This was my favorite photo I took. It was when it was getting dark and the composition was unintentional. I saw Half Dome and took a quick pic. The branches of the tree in front of me kind of match the Half Dome inverted, and then the trees and railing makes the entire photo look symmetrical and intentional.

![Half Dome at dusk](assets/Yosemite6.jpeg)
